Grant Standard Notes access to your keyring

To store your password securely, Standard Notes needs to save and retrieve passwords on your system keyring (libsecret).

Due to limitations of Linux keyring access, Standard Notes will be granted access to all your keyring entries. We will not read these values, but technically, we can. If you prefer to use local storage instead to store your keys, you may do so. You can add a local passcode to the app to encrypt your local storage.

To grant Standard Notes access to your keyring, visit the Standard Notes page in your software store an look for a Permissions button. Then, quit and relaunch the app.

Alternatively, you may run the following command in your terminal:
snap connect standard-notes:password-manager-service
